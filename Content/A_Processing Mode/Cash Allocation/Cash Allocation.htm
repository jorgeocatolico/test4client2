Cash Allocation

 

The following are key concepts of Cash Allocation:

  • There are two standard processes: Cash Allocation and Rebalancing, commonly referred to as CAR. It is possible to add other processes if required.
  • Cash Allocation processes are organised into standard processing phases which are executed in a pre-defined sequence.
  • Cash Allocation processes are driven through the configuration of the processing template and the setting of attributes associated to an entity.
  • A set of worktables are used to load information required by the process, hold data that does not change on a regular basis such as mappings and tolerances, and to hold the results of processing before being used in follow on processing, or for reporting.
  • pControl captures and processes orders and transactions for an entity which updates the asset balances, registry balances and internal account balances which are used by the Cash Allocation product to calculate an entity's liquidity position and actual investment position into its underlying assets for a given day or point in time.
  • The key balances that are maintained and used by the Cash Allocation process are investment holdings and internal account balances.
  • The output of the Cash Allocation process is a proposed order which can be used as the basis of a market order.
  • The Cash Allocation process understands the investment structure that an entity is part of and, for an individual fund, the process will 'wait' until all investors have been processed before continuing so that all cashflows are available.
  • The Cash Allocation process calculates the liquidity position (available cash) of an entity and also its actual, current allocation weights into underlying assets.
  • A target investment structure is defined in pControl (using Allocation Parameters, known as ALPs) and used to determine a set of proposed orders for an entity. pControl supports the notion of a 'Notional Sector' which minimises overheads associate with the maintenance of ALPs.
  • Different CAR methodologies can be used on an entity by fund basis for the generation of proposed orders.
  • Balancing / Default options are used to reduce the number of transactions that are sent to the market within an investment structure and also to keep a set of investment (balancing) options' actual allocations in line with their targets.
  • Other processes that support Cash Allocation include loading ALPs, creating registry orders and bulk cancellation of orders.

Portfolio Management - Web Mode

Portfolio Management is a complementary dashboard to the Cash Allocation and Rebalancing process. In processing mode, described above, CAR is run to establish start of day balances or undertake rebalancing and distributing any incoming cash, or apportioning outgoing cash to/from underlying fund structures, which can consist of one or many entities in a hierarchy with unlimited tiers, and multiple currencies. The 'Proposed Transactions' generated by the CAR process are displayed in both Processing mode and in the third panel of the Portfolio Management dashboard in the web view. These proposed transactions can be auto-approved when using CAR, to convert them into real orders.
