Multi Leg Derivatives

There are many types of financial instruments that can be classified as multi-leg derivatives, the majority of which can be classified as either forwards or swaps.

Forwards

A forward contract is a non-standard contract between two parties to buy or sell an asset at a specified price on a future date. A forward contract can be used for hedging or speculation. The party agreeing to buy the underlying asset in the future assumes a long position, and the party agreeing to sell the asset in the future assumes a short position. The price agreed upon is called the delivery price, which is equal to the forward price at the time the contract is entered into. Types of forwards include:

  • FX Forward Example
  • NDFs
  • Forward Rate Agreements

Swaps

Swaps are instruments whereby two parties exchange a series of cash flows over a period of time. Examples of swaps include:

  • Interest Rate Swap Example
  • Total Return Swap
  • Currency Swap
  • Equity Swap
  • Volatility Swap
  • Variance Swap
  • Commodity Swap
  • Dividend Swap
  • Asset Swap
  • Inflation Linked Swap

 

Note

There is a difference between multi-leg derivatives and multi-leg transactions. Multi-leg transactions typically form part of an overall trade strategy which contains more than one leg associated with a given order. Strips, Packs, Bundles and Calendar Spreads are examples of multi-leg transactions. In pControl, only multi-leg derivatives are catered for at this time.

[MLH057] Multi Leg Holding Validation - FX Forward Example Calculation
Example

To calculate an unrealised gain on a position, the formula is:

$Market\ Value\ -\ Cost\ =\ Unrealised\ Gain/Loss$

As an FX Forward has no cost, the formula is simplified to:

$Market\ Value\ =\ Unrealised\ Gain/Loss$

The below table represents data available in the U100: Investment Holdings worktable for an FX Forward. In this example, the fund manager has agreed a forward contract EUR vs USD to sell 1,000,000EUR for USD at a contractual price of 1.35 USD per EUR to settle in 6 months. At NAV date, the current EUR for USD forward rate to settle in 6 months is 1.3965 (the USD-EUR rate = 0.7160736).

The U100: Investment Holdings worktable holds two records that represent that FX forward position. One asset, or leg, with a corresponding long position of 1,350,000USD and another asset with a short position of -1,000,000EUR.

With a USD base currency at NAV date, the formula to calculate the Base Market Value of FX Forward is:

$Base\ Unrealised\ Gain/Loss\ = Trade\ Currency\ Quantity\ \times \left ( Contractual\ Forward\ Rate\ -\ Current\ Forward\ Rate \right )$

$1,000,000\ \times \left ( 1.34\ -\ 1.3965 \right ) \\ =\ -46,504$

Therefore, the Base Market Value of FX Forward = -46,504USD

Details of Validation

Component

Description

Business Description

Provides a net impact value of all legs of a multi leg asset and validates that impact against a tolerance.

Processing Detail

The following is required as part of the data set up:

  • L220: Instrument Type Mappings: Multi-Leg Instrument field to be selected in order to create the record in the A03: Holding Validation and the A17: Multi Leg Master Holding
  • U100: Investment Holdings: Multi-Leg Asset Code which is the common identifier for the security.

Other data requirements:

A17: Multi Leg Master Holding worktable

  • Asset Holding Impact Basis Points (Pm Adj Imp)

$Asset\ Hld\ Imp\ BP\ =\ \frac{Value\ Mvt\ -\ Purchases\ Value\ -\ Sales\ Value }{NAV}$

  • Asset Holding Impact Per Unit Impact (Pm Adj Imp PUI)

$Asset\ Hld\ Imp\ PUI\ =\ \frac{Value\ Mvt\ -\ Purchases\ Value\ -\ Sales\ Value }{Total\ Units\ on\ Issue}$

Note: The A17: Multi Leg Master Holding worktable supports the multi leg framework, where the A03: Holding Validation worktable lists each leg of the security separately, the A17: Multi Leg Master Holding worktable combines the legs and therefore the values and movements and displays a net impact.

The validation fails where A17.pm_adj_imp > L200.tolerance.

The validation fails where A17.pm_adj_imp_pui > L200.tolerance
