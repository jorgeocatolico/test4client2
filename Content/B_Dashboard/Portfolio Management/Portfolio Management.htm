Portfolio Management

Portfolio Management is a workbench supporting Cash Allocation and Rebalancing process (CAR). CAR is run in pControl's Processing Mode to establish start of day balances, or undertake rebalancing and distributing any incoming cash, or apportioning outgoing cash to/from underlying fund structures, which can consist of one or many entities in a hierarchy with unlimited tiers, and multiple currencies. The 'Proposed Transactions' generated by the CAR process are displayed in both Processing mode and in the third panel of the Portfolio Management workbench in the web view. These proposed transactions can be auto-approved when using CAR, to convert them into real orders.

Portfolio Management allows additional functionality. The Proposed Transactions can be presented as a list, and they can also be viewed according to different classifications, such as orders grouped by asset class, geographic region or by custodian. Grouping orders by additional classification criteria allows validation checks to be performed to ensure compliance with any overarching business rules, such as ensuring investments remain within given limits across an asset class. The Portfolio Manager has the discretion to approve or adjust pControl's calculated proposed transactions, prior to the approval process. During the modelling process, any breaches of compliance will be highlighted, allowing further changes to be made before approving. Hard breaches cannot be overridden, while soft breaches may be approved with an override.

Stages of the Cash Allocation and Portfolio Management process

 

Reference

Description

1

The Cash Allocation (CAR)and Portfolio Management process uses data from a variety of worktables. Some of this data is reasonably static, and does not need to be loaded every day, such as the U495, U496, U498, L290. Other worktables, such as U100, U108, U110 will usually receive data every day. Data may come from a variety of sources, files, tables, external systems, and will usually be fed in automatically. (In some circumstances, data may be manually entered into pControl).

Note: The ‘Data Manager’ product may be used to detect the arrival of new data files and process these into pControl, or other automation tools may be used.

2

CAR / Portfolio Management data is loaded into various worktables for storage and basic validation, (i.e. ensuring date fields are populated with valid dates, cash and unit fields contains numeric values, and valid pControl entity codes are used).

3

The CAR process is initiated, either manually or automatically.

Data from the various worktables is used as the inputs to the process. Various functions are executed during the processing phases, depending upon the configuration (i.e. selection of functions) on the process template.

Note/s: Reference numbers 4-7, and 11, are the processing phases of the CAR process.

4

The ‘Validation Checks’ phase performs checks to ensure it is valid to run CAR processing for this entity on this effective date. This may include checking if today is a business day or weekend; checking if CAR has already been run today.

5

The data load phase selects data from specified worktables based on the functions selected on the processing template, for use in the CAR process and calculations.

6

The ‘Validate Data’ phase performs checks to ensure the data available can successfully be used in the CAR process, such as checking the ALPs sum to 100%, or checking UOI, or liabilities mismatches.

7

During the 'Calculation' phase, cash and assets are reallocated according to the methodology and weights configured. Any 'spare cash' can also be reallocated where necessary. The output from this process and calculations is a set of Proposed Transactions.

8

Proposed Transactions generated during the 'calculation' phase are displayed in the Portfolio Management workbench. Investment Summary and Classifications are also displayed in their respective panels.

Investment Summary shows a summarised view of the CAR process including rolled up exceptions, total investment balances and order proposals.

Classifications detail all the classification compliance frameworks that are being tested. The users can view each compliance framework and any exceptions or out of tolerance / bound events.

Proposed transactions display all of the underlying order proposals, including investment compliance checking. Users can amend order proposals within this screen, validate and save changes.

Note: Proposed transactions can also be viewed in processing mode.

9

Proposed Transaction values can be reviewed via the Portfolio Management workbench. Values can be adjusted if required. Certain fields on the proposed transaction can also be altered, depending on a user's access, and the data view selected.

10

All post compliance rules will be triggered using the proposed orders. Any exception events raised at this point will be presented for further action. Orders are generated if there are no exceptions.

11

During the 'Process Ends' phase, orders are consolidated, i.e., where there are two or more approved transactions into the same asset, the orders are summed / netted to calculate a single application / redemption value into the asset. Orders are then exported outside of pControl.

12

Approved Cash Allocation orders are exported to the U570: Cash Allocation Orders, the U575: Classification Allocation or others.

13

The approval of proposed transactions in the Portfolio Management workbench, results in approved transaction records.

 

 Portfolio Management workbench

The workbench is made up of three panels:

  • The Investment Summary showing an entity's values pre and post CAR process;
  • Classifications showing the value of the proposed transactions when grouped by the configured classifications, (e.g., asset class or custodian);
  • Proposed Transactions i.e. a list of orders for each entity.

Using the active filtering

The Settings drop-down on the primary filter enables filtering by active and inactive entities. The 'Active' and 'Inactive' setting is part of the entity attribute which can be changed via the library tree.

The 'Active' and 'Inactive' filters also apply when selecting the Collection or Template options under the Entity drop-down. Refer to Collections for steps on how to create entity collections.

The data displayed will not be refreshed until <Search> has been selected.

 Proposed Order Status Streams

As the cash allocation process progresses through the phases of processing, there are many 'paths' that processing can take depending on the configuration of the entity and pControl static data, the data used in processing etc. The 'paths' in pControl are determined by status streams, status streams represent a predefined map of states and transitions between states.

There are two status streams, the Master Proposed Order Status Stream and the Proposed Order Status Stream.
