If the Portfolio Manager chooses to model the values of the proposed transactions (in the third panel), a validation option executes the classification and investment compliance checks. The classifications are updated to display the new values. Breaches of compliance limits, sector limits, or any other defined criteria are highlighted to the Manager as either 'hard' or 'soft'.

Proposed transactions with 'soft out of tolerance' / 'soft out of bounds' or no warnings can be approved by the Manager. However 'hard out of tolerance' / 'hard out of bounds' will prevent the proposed transaction being approved, until the proposed transactions have been modelled to values which produce no, or soft exceptions only.

Note

Refer to Classification Configuration for information on creating, editing and deleting classification sectors; and to manage the associated Classification Parameters.

Proposed Orders with soft warnings can still be approved via an override. Proposed orders with hard warnings cannot be approved until the order value is adjusted to be within the bounds and tolerances.

 Managing Exceptions on Sectors without Holdings

Sectors are typically populated with the values from a plan's assets when they, or their holdings, are associated with an asset of that plan. When a sector is not populated with the values from a plan, the sector will not be displayed in the classifications panel of the Portfolio Management dashboard. These sectors are referred to as not-held sectors.

When a fund manager requires to perform compliance checks and manage compliance breaches on an aggregate sector, a dummy investment ALR needs to be created. This dummy investment ALR is identified as "is_classification_sector":

  1. The not-held sector's classification ALR, and its parents, are created and displayed.
  2. Compliance breaches can be managed by overriding the "is_classification_sector" on the status stream on the proposed transactions panel.

The stand-in ALR is the mechanism by which pControl can determine when a not-held sector is to be displayed for a plan. For example, a classification may exist which has two aggregate sectors that have no holding sector descendants that are associated with the plan's assets, but only one of the not-held sectors is to be displayed. Compliance checks can now be applied to the sector and any exceptions applied against the stand-in ALR which can subsequently be transitioned accordingly.
