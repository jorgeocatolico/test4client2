Reallocation of Value

Usually, the value of an entity is calculated by summing all the investment holdings of that entity. However, in some cases, certain investment holdings may need to be included in the value of a different entity to the one that actually holds the investment.

Reallocation may be required for:

  • Strategic or tactical asset allocation reasons
  • Derivatives held in an overlay portfolio
  • Profit / loss from hedging which is held in a hedging portfolio
  • Any isolated asset value held in a special portfolio.

Understanding Reallocation of Value

Investment Holdings can be reallocated to one or more entities, as long as those entities are all within the same plan. The reallocation details are recorded in the L290: Effective Exposure Reallocation worktable, and the U310: Exposure Reallocation Source worktable. When processing is run, the values of the assets listed in these worktables are transferred from one entity to another.

Reallocation of Value example
Example

 

In this example, ABC Account has 11 portfolio entities. The Non-US Equity entity has investment holdings in Emerging Markets, TBCAM, SSGA World Ex US, Acadian Managed Volatility Equity, SSGA S&P 500 Index Fund and DFA Intl Small Company.

The Portfolio Manager decides to reallocate the value of the SSGA World Ex US shares from the Non-US Equity entity to the Global Equity entity. As reallocation is not a transfer of ownership, the Global Low Volatility entity will never hold the title to the SSGA World Ex US.

Note

Reallocation is not a transfer of ownership. The title of the asset remains with the 'Source' entity, as listed in the L290: Effective Exposure Reallocation worktable.
