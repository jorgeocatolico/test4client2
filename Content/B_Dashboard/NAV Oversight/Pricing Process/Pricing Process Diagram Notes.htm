Pricing Process Explained

 

Reference

Description

1

NAV Oversight data including purchase and sale transactions, cash movements, unit and investment holdings, and general ledger data are obtained from a variety of external sources. This data is fed into pControl automatically. (In some circumstances, data is manually entered into pControl).

Note: The ‘Data Manager’ product may be used to detect the arrival of new data files and process these into pControl, or other automation tools may be used.

2

NAV Oversight data is loaded into various worktables for storage and basic validation, (i.e. ensuring date fields are populated with valid dates, Index close field contains numeric values, valid pControl entity codes are used etc)

3

The NAV Oversight process is initiated, either manually or automatically.

Data from the various worktables is used as the inputs to the process. Various functions are executed during the processing phases, depending upon the configuration (i.e. selection of functions) on the process template.

Note/s: Reference numbers 4-15 are the processing phases of the NAV Oversight process.

Coloured icons shown next to a function code, indicates a separate entity exists containing further detail of the validation check including business descriptions and formula.

4

Versioning phase determines whether multiple versions of the data are allowed or not, depending upon the configuration of the process functions. If versioning is not allowed, subsequent processing will overwrite the data from the first processing run. Other versioning functions can also be selected.

5

Initialisation phase checks if the process has been run previously. If not, the process will run in ‘initialisation’ mode, meaning any functions which require ‘previous day’s data’ will not be performed.

6

The ‘Validity checks’ phase performs checks to ensure it is valid to run NAV Oversight processing for this entity on this effective date. This may include checking if today is a business day or weekend; Checking if Expected Pricing has already been run today.

7

The data load phase selects data from specified worktables based on the functions selected on the processing template, for use in the Expected Pricing process and calculations.

8

Pre Calc validations are checks performed on the data loaded during ‘data load phase’. In this example the functions (checks) selected include HLD003, TXN001, ACC001, VA011. Each of these function codes refer to a separate validation entity which contains both a business description and formula of the check performed.

Note: The four checks shown in diagram are only a small selection of the checks available for selection on the process template.

9

The calculation of assets including GAV calculations or component adjustments are performed in the ‘Calculate Assets’ phase. The exact calculations performed depend on the functions selected on the process template.

10

The calculation of liabilities such as fees and taxes is performed during the ‘Calculate liabilities’ phase. In this example, Fees Provision, Tax Provision and Performance Fees will be calculated.

11

The calculation of the valuation or prices is performed during the ‘Calculate Valuations / Prices phase including NAV and GAV prices, (i.e NAV / units and GAV / units).

Note: The four functions shown in diagram are only a small selection of the functions available on the process template.

12

Post Calc validations are checks performed on the data produced in the ‘Calculate Valuations / Prices’ phase. The output of some of these calculations including CAT001 and CAT002 are displayed on the NMA report.

The VAxxx functions are special function codes which must be selected (check boxes ticked in the process template) in order for the relevant Analysis report to be produced.

13

The NMA, OMAS and CPAS reports are produced during the ‘Post Calc Validation’ phase when the relevant functions are selected. This will be displayed in one of the tabs in the second panel of the Price Control Summary view, and the NMA report is also available in the U780 worktable.

14

During the ‘Data Exports’ phase, the outputs of the Expected Pricing processes are distributed to various worktables depending on the function codes selected on the process template.

15

The NAV Oversight process can be configured to be automatically approved and the control record ‘released’, which will trigger subsequent dependent processes. Alternatively, the process be configured to be automatically approved only, which will require manual release of the control record.

16

When Expected Pricing processing has been completed successfully other pControl processes, which are dependent on the outputs of the Expected Pricing process, can commence. These processes may be initiated manually, or automatically such as through the approval phase, chained processing, or STP methods.

17

The Analysis reports produced in step 13 can be reviewed, manually to determine if the movement is in line with expectations.
