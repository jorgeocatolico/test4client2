Cash Allocation and Rebalancing

The following are key concepts of Cash Allocation and Rebalancing:

  • There are three standard processes: Cash Allocation and Rebalancing, commonly referred to as CAR; and Start of Day. The Start of Day process utilises the same process flow and template as CAR.
  • Cash Allocation processes are organised into standard processing phases which are executed in a pre-defined sequence.
  • Cash Allocation processes are driven through the configuration of the processing template and the setting of attributes associated to an entity.
  • A set of worktables are used to load data used by the CAR process, store static data such as tolerances and target weights, and to hold results of processing for other downstream calculations or reporting.
  • pControl captures and processes orders and transactions for an entity, which updates the asset balances, registry balances and internal account balances. The CAR process uses these up to date balances to calculate an entity's liquidity position and actual investment position of its underlying assets for a given day or point in time.
  • The key balances that are maintained and used by the Cash Allocation process are investment holdings and internal account balances.
  • The output of the CAR process is a proposed order which can be used as the basis of a market order.
  • The Cash Allocation process understands multi-level interfund investment structures. The CAR process for an individual fund, the process will 'wait' until all investors (originator entities) have been processed before commencing so that all cashflows into the entity have been calculated.
  • The Cash Allocation process calculates the liquidity position (available cash, inclusive of payables/receivables) and current allocation weights to underlying assets for the entity.
  • A target investment structure is defined in pControl (using Allocation Parameters, known as ALPs) and used to calculate a set of proposed orders for an entity. The allocation parameters maintain the target weights, tolerances and bounds of underlying assets for a given entity. The CAR process also supports notional and model portfolios, which minimise the overhead associated with the maintenance of allocation parameters.
  • CAR supports a number of different methodologies for calculating proposed orders. CAR methodologies can be defined on an entity by entity basis.
  • Balancing / Default options are used to reduce the number of transactions that are sent to the market within an investment structure and also to keep a set of investment (balancing) options' actual allocations in line with their targets.
  • Additional processes that support Cash Allocation include loading allocation parameters, loading classification parameters, loading look-through data, revaluation of exposure, and creating registry orders.

Portfolio Management

The Portfolio Management dashboard is complementary to the Cash Allocation and Rebalancing processes. The PM dashboard supports advanced features such as classification structures and compliance/exposure monitoring. CAR is run to establish start of day balances or undertake cash allocation/rebalancing. The Proposed Transactions generated by the CAR process are displayed in the Portfolio Management dashboard and workflow is managed via the dashboard. These proposed orders generated have 'real time' impact on exposure where classification structures exist on an entity.
